Gauge Invariance

Magnetic Fields

Since the divergence of $\vec{B}$ is zero, $\vec{B}$ is the curl of another field.

$ \begin{eqnarray} \vec{B} & = & \nabla \times \vec{A} \end{eqnarray} $

Gauge Transformation

$ \begin{eqnarray} \vec{A} & \rightarrow & \vec{A} + \nabla \phi \end{eqnarray} $

$\vec{B}$ computed from $\vec{A}$ is the same (guage invariant).

Lagrangian

$ \begin{eqnarray} L & = & \frac{1}{2} m v^{2} + e \vec{v} \cdot \vec{A} \end{eqnarray} $

$L$ can only be constructed from $\vec{A}$, not $\vec{B}$.

Action only changes by an amount that depends on the endpoints. But it will have no effect on the equations of motion.

Canonical Momentum

$ \begin{eqnarray} \vec{p} & = & m \vec{v} + e \vec{A} \end{eqnarray} $

Euler-Lagrange equations reproduce the Lorentz force law (gauge invariant).

Hamiltonian

$ \begin{eqnarray} H & = & \frac{1}{2 m} (\vec{p} - e \vec{A})^{2} \\ & = & \frac{1}{2} m v^{2} \end{eqnarray} $

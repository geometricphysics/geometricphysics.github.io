Symmetry and Conservation Laws

Symmetry

A symmetry is an active transformation that does not change the value of the Lagrangian.

We say that the Lagrangian is invariant under the transformation.

We say that the Lagrangian is symmetric with respect to the transformation.

Continuous Transformations

Transformations that can be composed from infinitesimal transformations.

These transformations depend on a continuous parameter.

Symmetry of the Lagrangian can be checked for an infinitesimal transformation; the change in the Lagragian for the (first order) infinitesimal transformation must be zero.

General Symmetries

Consider the infinitesimal transformation

$ \begin{eqnarray} \delta q_{i} & = & f_{i}(q) \delta \end{eqnarray} $

The change in the Lagrangian is

$ \begin{eqnarray} \delta L & = & \sum_{i} (\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q_{i}}} \delta \dot{q_{i}} + \frac{\partial L}{\partial q_{i}} \delta q_{i}) \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Along a trajectory, the Euler-Lagrange equations are satisfied

$ \begin{eqnarray} \frac{d}{d t} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q_{i}}} & = & \frac{\partial L}{\partial q_{i}} \end{eqnarray} $

Hence

$ \begin{eqnarray} \delta L & = & \sum_{i} (\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q_{i}}} \delta \dot{q_{i}} + \frac{d}{d t} (\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q_{i}}}) \delta q_{i}) \\ & = & \frac{d}{d t} \sum_{i} (\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q_{i}}} \delta q_{i}) \\ & = & \frac{d}{d t} \sum_{i} (p_{i} f_i{q}) \delta \\ \end{eqnarray} $

If $L$ is symmetric with respect to the transformation then $\delta L = 0$, and since $\delta > 0$ then there is a conserved quantity

$ \begin{eqnarray} Q & = & \sum_{i} p_{i} f_{i}(q) \end{eqnarray} $

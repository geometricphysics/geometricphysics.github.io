Multi Particle Systems

Angular Momentum

Theorem. If the total linear momentum of a system of particles is zero, the total angular momentum is independent of the point about which the moments are taken.

Proof. Let $\vec{p}_{i}$ be the linear momentum of the $i$th particle.
We are given that the total linear momentum of the system of particles is zero:

$\sum_{i} \vec{p}_{i} = 0$
Let $\vec{r}_{i}$ be the displacement from the origin of the $i$th particle. Then the angular momentum of the $i$th particle about the origin is
$l_{i} = \vec{r}_{i} \wedge \vec{p}_{i}$.
The total angular momentum of the system of particles about the origin is
$\begin{eqnarray*} L & = & \sum_{i} l_{i} \\ & = & \sum_{i} \vec{r}_{i} \wedge \vec{p}_{i}\end{eqnarray*}$
The total angular momentum of the system of particles about a point $A$ with displacement $\vec{a}$ from the origin is
$\begin{eqnarray*} L_{A} & = & \sum_{i} (\vec{r}_{i} - \vec{a}) \wedge \vec{p}_{i} \\ & = & \sum_{i} \vec{r}_{i} \wedge \vec{p}_{i} - \sum_{i} \vec{a} \wedge \vec{p}_{i} \\ & = & L - \vec{a} \wedge \sum_{i} \vec{p}_{i} \\ & = & L.\blacksquare\end{eqnarray*}$

Theorem. The rate of change of the total angular momentum of a system of particles about a fixed point is equal to the sum of the torques about that point.

Proof. Let the fixed point be labelled $A$ and have a displacement $\vec{a}$ from the origin.
We are given that the point $A$ is fixed:

$\Large\frac{d}{dt}$$\vec{a} = 0$
Let $\vec{r}_{i}$ and $\vec{p}_{i}$ be the displacement from the origin and linear momentum of the $i$th particle respectively.
The total angular momentum of the system of partices about the point $A$ is
$L_{A} = \sum_{i} (\vec{r}_{i} - \vec{a}) \wedge \vec{p}_{i}$
Differentiating with respect to time gives the rate of change of the total angular momentum:
$\begin{eqnarray*} \frac{d}{dt} L_{A} & = & \frac{d}{dt} \sum_{i} (\vec{r}_{i} - \vec{a}) \wedge \vec{p}_{i} \\ & = & \sum_{i} (\vec{v}_{i} - \frac{d}{dt} \vec{a}) \wedge \vec{p}_{i} + \sum_{i} (\vec{r}_{i} - \vec{a}) \wedge \frac{d}{dt} \vec{p}_{i} \\ & = & 0 + \sum_{i} (\vec{r}_{i} - \vec{a}) \wedge \vec{F}_{i} \\ & = & \sum_{i} N_{i, A} \\ & = & N_{A}.\blacksquare\end{eqnarray*}$

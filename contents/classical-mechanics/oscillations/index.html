Oscillations

Simple Harmonic Motion

$f_{spring} = -k \times extension$ (Hooke's Law for an ideal or linear spring).

k is a positive scalar called the spring constant.

The minus sign causes the force to accelerate particle in the direction of the equilibrium position, so the force $f_{spring}$ is called a restoring force.

Potential Energy

The stored elastic potential energy is the work done by a force against the spring to cause a displacement.

$ \begin{eqnarray} U(x) & = & \int_{0}^{x} \mathbf{f}_{external} \cdot d\mathbf{x} \\ & = & - \int_{0}^{x} \mathbf{f}_{spring} \cdot d\mathbf{x} \\ & = & - \int_{0}^{x} - k \mathbf{x} \cdot d\mathbf{x} \\ & = & \frac{k \mathbf{x}^{2}}{2} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Kinematics

Period, $T$

Frequency, $f = 1 / T$

Angular Frequency, $\omega = \tau / T = \tau f$

Let $m$ be the mass of a particle attached to a spring with spring constant $k$.

The equation of motion of the particle is

$ \begin{eqnarray} m \mathbf{\ddot{x}} & = & - k \mathbf{x} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

The general solution is

$ \begin{eqnarray} \mathbf{x} & = & \mathbf{A} sin (\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} t) + \mathbf{B} cos (\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} t) \\ \end{eqnarray} $

So the angular frequency, $\omega$, is given by

$ \begin{eqnarray} \omega & = & \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Pendulum

A simple pendulum consists of a weight of mass $m$ attached to a string or massless rod that swings without friction, about the vertical equilibrium position. The restoring force is provided by gravity.

If $L$ is the length of the pendulum, $m$ is the mass of the weight, $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity, and $\theta$ is the angular displacement of the pendulum from vertical then

$ \begin{eqnarray} T cos \theta & = & m g \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} T sin \theta & = & - m L \ddot{\theta} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

For small displacements, $sin \theta \approx \theta$ and $cos \theta \approx 1$, giving

$ \begin{eqnarray} \ddot{\theta} & = & - \frac{g}{L} \theta \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$\theta$ performs simple harmonic motion with period

$ \begin{eqnarray} T & = & \tau \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

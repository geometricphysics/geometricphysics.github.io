Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

Orbits and Kepler's Laws

Kepler's First Law

Each planet's orbit about the Sun is an ellipse.

The Sun's center is always located at one focus of the orbital ellipse.

Kepler's Second Law

The imaginary line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps equal areas of space during equal time intervals as the planet orbits.

Kepler's Third Law

The squares of the orbital periods are directly proportional to the cubes of the semi-major axes of their orbits.

Definition: Perihelion

The point of nearest approach.

Definition: Aphelion

The point of greatest separation.

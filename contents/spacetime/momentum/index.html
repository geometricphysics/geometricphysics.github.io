Momentum and Mass

Mass

We will use a very restricted argument to deduce the dependence of the inertial mass on the relative velocity. Using the result as a conjecture, we proceed to find a more general derivation.

Consider a glancing collision between a sphere at rest in Alice's frame and a sphere at rest in Bob's frame.

We make the collision glancing so that the transverse velocity imparted to each sphere is very small compared to the relative velocity $v$ of the two inertial frames.

By symmetry, the spheres will have equal and opposite transverse velocities in their respective frames of magnitude $u_{\perp}$.

Suppose that the mass of the moving sphere, $m$ is a function of it's rest value $m_0$ as a result of the relative speed of the two frames. By conservation of the transverse momentum we have

$ \begin{eqnarray} m_0 u_{\perp} & = & \frac{m u_{\perp}}{\gamma(v)(1 \pm v \, u_{\perp})} \end{eqnarray} $, where the choice of sign depends whether the collision is being examined from Alice or Bob's frame.

Considering the limit of an increasingly glancing blow so that $v \, u_{\perp} \ll 1$, we obtain

$ \begin{eqnarray} m & = & \gamma(v) m_0 \end{eqnarray} $.

Velocity

Velocity Transformation

Using the Lorentz Transformation we can derive the transformation laws for the components of ordinary velocity.

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_x' & = & \frac{dx'}{dt'} & = & \frac{\gamma(dx - v \, dt)}{\gamma(dt - v \, dx)} & = & \frac{u_x - v}{1 - v \, u_x} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_y' & = & \frac{dy'}{dt'} & = & \frac{dy}{\gamma(dt - v \, dx)} & = & \frac{u_y}{\gamma(1 - v \, u_x)} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_z' & = & \frac{dz'}{dt'} & = & \frac{dz}{\gamma(dt - v \, dx)} & = & \frac{u_z}{\gamma(1 - v \, u_x)} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Notice that in the limit $v \rightarrow 0$ we recover the Galilean Transformation for velocities.

The inverse (velocity addition) transformations are found by interchanging primed and unprimed symbols and the replacement $v \rightarrow -v$:

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_x & = & \frac{u_x' + v}{1 + v \, u_x'} \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_y & = & \frac{u_y'}{\gamma(1 + v \, u_x')} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} u_z & = & \frac{u_z'}{\gamma(1 + v \, u_x')} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

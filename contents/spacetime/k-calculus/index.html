k-Calculus

The Big Picture

Firstly, k-Calculus is not actually calculus in the sense of infinitesimals and sums. We're using the more general meaning of the word as a procedure by which we calculate things. In particular we are talking about a procedure for determining the coordinates of an event. What's special about k-Calculus is that it avoids the intermediate step of creating a coordinate lattice with associated synchronized clocks. By using the more fundamental technique of measuring the elapsed time of an out-and-back signal, we hope to make the underlying physical principles more obvious.

In addition, we will seek ways to make the invariants manifest and take a geometric approach that avoids special coordinate systems, again to make the underlying physics more obvious.

The name k-Calculus comes from a function called $k$ that we invent that captures the assumption that we can't determine absolute velocity and that only relative velocity matters.

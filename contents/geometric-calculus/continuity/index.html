Functions, Limits and Continuity

Limit of a Function

Let the value $\phi(x)$ be defined and single valued for all values of $x$ near $x = a$ with the possible exception of $x = a$.

When we say $L$ is the limit of $\phi$ as $x$ approaches $a$, we mean that for any positive number $\epsilon$ we can find a positive number $\delta$ such that $| \phi(x) - L | < \epsilon$ whenever $0 < |x-a| < \delta$.

Continuity

When we say that a function $\phi$ with argument $x$ is continuous at $x=a$ we mean

$ \begin{eqnarray} \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \phi(x) & = & \phi(a) \end{eqnarray} $

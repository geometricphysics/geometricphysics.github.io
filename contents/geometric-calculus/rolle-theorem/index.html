Rolle's Theorem

Theorem

If $f(x)$ is continuous on the closed interval $[a,b]$ and differentiable on the open interval $(a,b)$, and if $f(a) = f(b) = 0$, then there exists a point $c$ in $(a,b)$ such that $f'(c) = 0$.

Geometric Interpretation

There exists a point $[c, f(c)]$ where the tangent line at this point is horizontal.

Proof

This proof uses a Theorem on Continuity, which takes us into Analysis.

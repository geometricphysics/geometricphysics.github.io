Mean Value Theorem

Mathematical Prolog

When considering functions over the field $\mathbb{R}$, this statement is the Mean Value Theorem.

Physical Prolog

The statement of this theorem is called the Law of the Mean.

Theorem

If $f(x)$ is continuous on the closed interval $[a,b]$ and differentiable on the open interval $(a,b)$, then there exists a point $c$ in $(a,b)$ such that

$ \begin{eqnarray*} \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} & = & f'(c) \end{eqnarray*} $

Geometric Interpretation

There exists a point $[c, f(c)]$ where the tangent line at this point is parallel to the line that passes through the points $[a, f(a)]$ and $[b, f(b)]$.

Geometric Interpretation

There exists a point $[c, f(c)]$ where the tangent line at this point is parallel to the line that passes through the points $[a, f(a)]$ and $[b, f(b)]$.

Proof

This proof uses Rolle's theorem.

Define a function $F(x)$ that satisfies the conditions for Rolle's theorem:

$ \begin{eqnarray*} F(x) & = & f(x) - f(a) - (x - a) \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \end{eqnarray*} $

$F(a) = F(b) = 0$, and $F(x)$ is continuous on the closed interval $[a,b]$ and differentiable on the open interval $(a,b)$.

Differentiating:

$ \begin{eqnarray*} F'(x) & = & f'(x) - \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \end{eqnarray*} $

Applying Rolle's theorem:

$ \begin{eqnarray*} 0 & = & f'(c) - \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \end{eqnarray*} $

Fluid Mechanics

The study of fluids and their dynamics

  • Density
  • Pressure
  • Force Density
  • Hydrostatic Pressure

Density

$ \begin{eqnarray} density & = & \frac{mass}{volume} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Pressure

$ \begin{eqnarray} pressure & = & \frac{force}{area} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Force Density

$ \begin{eqnarray} \Delta F_{x} & = & P(x) A - P(x + \Delta x) A \\ \frac{\Delta F_{x}}{\Delta V} & = & - \frac{[P(x + \Delta x) - P(x)]}{\Delta x} \\ f_{x} & = & - \frac{\partial P}{\partial x} \\ \mathbf{f} & = & - \nabla P \\ \end{eqnarray} $

Hydrostatic Pressure

Consider a horizontal massless plate of width $w$ and length $l$ at depth $h$ in fluid. Assume that the density of the fluid, $\rho$, is uniform.

Let P(h) be the pressure at depth $h$. For the plate to be in equilibrium, we must have

$ \begin{eqnarray} P(h) w l & = & P(0) w l + \rho w l h g \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} P(h) & = & P(0) + \rho g h \\ \end{eqnarray} $

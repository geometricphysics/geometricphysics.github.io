Dirac delta distribution

Definition

The Dirac delta distribution is defined by the following properties:

Spike

$ \begin{eqnarray} \delta(x) = 0, x \neq 0 \end{eqnarray} $,

Normalized

$ \begin{eqnarray} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dx \delta(x) = 1 \end{eqnarray} $,

Selection

$ \begin{eqnarray} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dx f(x) \delta(x) = f(0) \end{eqnarray} $

Approximation

The Dirac delta distribution may be approximated by a variety of functions:

Rectangle

$ \delta_n(x) = \left\{ \begin{eqnarray} & n &, -1/2n \lt x \lt 1/2n \\ & 0 & , otherwise \end{eqnarray} \right. $

The integral properties follow easily from the definition.

Gaussian

$ \delta_n(x) = \begin{eqnarray} exp(-n^2 x^2) \end{eqnarray} $

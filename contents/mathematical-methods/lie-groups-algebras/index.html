Lie Groups, Lie Algebras

Lie Group

Our interest will be in parameterized coordinate transformations:

$ \begin{eqnarray} \Primed{x}_i & = & f_i(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n ; a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_r) \end{eqnarray} $

where $i = 1, 2, \ldots, n.$

e.g., The continuous parameters $(a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_r)$ may be angles (rotations) or relative velocity (Lorentz transformation).

Considering the effect of such an r-parameter transformation on a field $F(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)$:

$ \begin{eqnarray} d F & = & \sum_{j=1}^{n} dx_j \frac{\partial F}{\partial x_j} \\ & = & \sum_{j=1}^{n} \left( \sum_{i=1}^{r} da_i \frac{\partial f_j}{\partial a_i} \right) \frac{\partial F}{\partial x_j} \\ & = & \sum_{i=1}^{r} da_i \left( \sum_{j=1}^{n} \frac{\partial f_j}{\partial a_i} \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j} \right) F \\ \end{eqnarray} $

The differential operator corresponding to the infinitesimal $da_i$ is defined to be

$ \begin{eqnarray} X_i & = & \sum_{j=1}^{n} \frac{\partial f_j}{\partial a_i} \frac{\partial}{\partial x_j} \end{eqnarray} $

There will be a differential operator corresponding to each parameter in the transformation.

Lie Algebra

We can form an $r$-dimensional vector space using the differential operators over some field $\Field{F}$.

If we then add a vector-valued multiplication operation to the vector space we have an algebra.

Imposing some particular rules on the multiplication operation, we arrive at a Lie Algebra.

A Lie Algebra is a vector space $V$ over a field $\Field{F}$ together with a binary operation $[\cdot,\cdot] : V \times V \rightarrow V$, called the Lie bracket, which has the following properties:

  1. Bilinearity.

    $ \begin{eqnarray} [ax+by, z] & = & a[x,z] + b[y,z] \end{eqnarray} $

    $ \begin{eqnarray} [z, ax+by] & = & a[z,x] + b[z,y] \end{eqnarray} $

    $\forall a, b \in \Field{F}$ and $\forall x, y, z \in V$.

  2. Jacobi Identity.

    $ \begin{eqnarray} [x,[y,z]] + [y,[z,x]] + [z,[x,y]] & = & 0 \end{eqnarray} $

    $\forall x, y, z \in V$.

  3. Asymmetry.

    $ \begin{eqnarray} [x,y] & = & -[y,x] \end{eqnarray} $

    $\forall x, y \in V$.

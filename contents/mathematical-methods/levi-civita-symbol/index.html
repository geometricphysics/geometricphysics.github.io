Levi-Civita Symbol in 2 dimensions

Definition

$ \varepsilon_{ij} = \left \{ \begin{array}{11} +1 & \mbox{if $(i,j)$ is $(1, 2)$,} \\ -1 & \mbox{if $(i,j)$ is $(2, 1)$,} \\ 0 & \mbox{otherwise: $i = j$.} \end{array} \right. $

Properties

Consider $\varepsilon_{ij} \varepsilon_{mn}$

It has

  • $2^{4} = 16$ components,
  • $2$ components are $+1$,
  • $2$ components are $-1$,
  • and the remaining $(16 - 4) = 12$ components are zero.
$i$$j$$m$$n$$\varepsilon_{ij} \varepsilon_{mn}$
$1$$2$$1$$2$$+1$
$2$$1$$2$$1$$+1$
$1$$2$$2$$1$$-1$
$2$$1$$1$$2$$-1$

We get $+1$ when $i=m$ and $j=n$, which is $\delta_{im} \delta_{jn}$.
We get $-1$ when $i=n$ and $j=m$, which is $\delta_{in} \delta_{jm}$.

This suggests the following identity, which may be verified

$ \begin{eqnarray} \varepsilon_{ij} \varepsilon_{mn} & = & \delta_{im} \delta_{jn} - \delta_{in} \delta_{jm} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

This result could also be written as a $2 \times 2$ matrix determinant.

Contraction

Contraction is the operation of setting two indices equal and summing over all possible values of the index.

$ \begin{eqnarray} \sum_{j} \varepsilon_{ij} \varepsilon_{mj} & = & \sum_{j} ( \delta_{im} \delta_{jj} - \delta_{ij} \delta_{jm} ) \\ & = & 2 \delta_{im} - \delta_{i1} \delta_{1m} - \delta_{i2} \delta_{2m} \\ & = & \delta_{im} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

$ \begin{eqnarray} \sum_{i} \sum_{j} \varepsilon_{ij} \varepsilon_{ij} & = & \sum_{i} \delta_{ii} \\ & = & \sum_{i} 1 \\ & = & 2 \\ \end{eqnarray} $

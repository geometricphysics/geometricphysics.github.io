Clocks

Clock Definition

A clock is a physical device for recording the occurrence of an event at the position of the clock as a number.

The output of the clock is a list of numbers, one for each event.

Unless the clock is reset, the list of numbers increases monotonically.

The number recorded is called the time of the event according to the clock at the position of the event.

Clock Implementation

A clock is realized by forcing a naturally occurring damped oscillator to resonate with a macroscopic frequency source by tuning the frequency source to deliver maximum amplitude. A counter is then used against the macroscopic frequency source.

Good Clock

A good clock is one that when compared to other clocks, in the limit of zero separation, records the same interval between events.

Clock Hypothesis (Ideal Clock Definition)

The rate of an ideal clock moving relative to an inertial frame depends only upon its instantaneous speed relative to the inertial frame.

Commentary

This definition of a clock includes that act of measurement so that a clock is a complete device for measuring the time of events. It's not good enough to say that a pendulum is a clock; we must include the observer who is counting the pendulim swings and correlating the number of swings to events in the neighborhood.

de Broglie Matter Wave

Matter Waves

Louis de Broglie hypothesized in his 1923 PhD thesis that all particles have an associated wave with the wavelength related to the momentum by

$ \begin{eqnarray} p & = & \frac{h}{\lambda} \end{eqnarray} $

de Broglie relations

Special relativity establishes the following relationship between the energy, momentum and rest mass of a massive particle

$ \begin{eqnarray} E^{2} & = & (pc)^{2} + (m c^{2})^{2} \\ \end{eqnarray} $

For a particle such as a photon, which travels at the speed of light, the rest mass is zero but the particle still has momentum and the energy is

$ \begin{eqnarray} E & = & p c \\ \end{eqnarray} $

But, according to the photoelectric effect

$ \begin{eqnarray} E & = & h \nu & = & \frac{h c}{\lambda} \end{eqnarray} $

So, for a photon

$ \begin{eqnarray} p & = & \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{h}{2 \pi} \frac{2 \pi}{\lambda} = \hbar k \end{eqnarray} $

de Broglie postulated that the same realtion holds true for massive particles.

Experimental Verification

Most famous is the diffraction of electrons off a nickel surface by Davisson and Germer in 1927.

Vectors and Linear Spaces

Inverse

Show that for any $u \in \mathit{Cl}_2$, $u \bar{u} = \bar{u} u \in \mathbb{R}$, and that $u$ is invertible, if $u \bar{u} \neq 0$, with inverse

$ \begin{eqnarray} u^{-1} = \frac{\bar{u}}{u \bar{u}} \end{eqnarray} $.

Let $u = u^0 + u^1 e_1 + u^2 e_2 + u^{12} e_{12}$. Then

$ \begin{eqnarray} \bar{u} & = & \sum_k (-1)^{\frac{1}{2} k (k+1)} {\langle u \rangle}_k = u^0 - u^1 e_1 - u^2 e_2 - u^{12} e_{12} \end{eqnarray} $.

$ \begin{eqnarray} u \bar{u} & = & (u^0 + u^1 e_1 + u^2 e_2 + u^{12} e_{12})(u^0 - u^1 e_1 - u^2 e_2 - u^{12} e_{12}) \\ & = & (u^0)^2 - (u^1)^2 - (u^2)^2 + (u^{12})^2 \end{eqnarray} $.

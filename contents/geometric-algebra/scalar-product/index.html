Scalar Product

Definition

For $k$-blades $\Blade{A} = \Vector{a_1} \wedge \ldots \wedge \Vector{a_k}$ and $\Blade{B} = \Vector{b_1} \wedge \ldots \wedge \Vector{b_k}$ and scalars $\alpha$ and $\beta$, the scalar product $\ast \, \colon \, \bigwedge^{k} \RealField^{n} \times \bigwedge^{k} \RealField^{n} \rightarrow \RealField$ is defined as

$ \begin{eqnarray} \alpha \ast \beta & = & \alpha \beta \\ \Blade{A} \ast \Blade{B} & = & \left| \begin{array}{cccc} \Vector{a}_1 \cdot \Vector{b}_k & \Vector{a}_1 \cdot \Vector{b}_{k-1} & \cdots & \Vector{a}_1 \cdot \Vector{b}_1 \\ \Vector{a}_2 \cdot \Vector{b}_k & \Vector{a}_2 \cdot \Vector{b}_{k-1} & \cdots & \Vector{a}_2 \cdot \Vector{b}_1 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \Vector{a}_k \cdot \Vector{b}_k & \Vector{a}_k \cdot \Vector{b}_{k-1} & \cdots & \Vector{a}_k \cdot \Vector{b}_1 \end{array} \right| \end{eqnarray} $

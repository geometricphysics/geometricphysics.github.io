Matrix representation of $\mathit{Cl}_2$

Isomorphism

$ \begin{eqnarray} 1 & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{crcr} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $, $ \begin{eqnarray} e_1 & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{crcr} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $, $ \begin{eqnarray} e_2 & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{crcr} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $, $ \begin{eqnarray} e_{12} & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{crcr} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $.

For arbitrary elements the above correspondences mean that

$ \begin{eqnarray} a^0 + a^1 e_1 + a^2 e_2 + a^{12} e_{12} & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} a^0 + a^1 & a^2 + a^{12} \\ a^2 - a^{12} & a^0 - a^1 \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $,

and

$ \begin{eqnarray} \frac{1}{2} [(a + d) + (a - d) e_1 + (b + c) e_2 + (b - c) e_{12}] & \simeq & \left[ \begin{array}{cccc} a & b \\ c & d \end{array} \right] \end{eqnarray} $.

Reverse

The transpose of a matrix corresponds to the reverse of the multivector.

Clifford-conjugate

The complementary matrix matrix corresponds to the Clifford-conjugate.

Thermal Physics

Thermal Energy

The energy of a system due to random motion of its molecules.

Heat

Heat is thermal energy that is in transit.

Temperature

A macroscopic measure of the thermal energy in a system.

Temperature Scales

Kelvin, Celsius, Farenheit

Temperature Change

The temperature increase of the system is proportial to the heat going in

$Q = m s \Delta T$

where

  • $m$ is the mass
  • $s$ is the specific heat

Phase Change

When there is a phase change, the temperature does not change. The heat exchanged is proportial to the mass

$Q = m L$

where

  • $m$ is the mass
  • $L$ is the latent heat of transformation

Thermal Expansion

When the temperature changes, the system may change.

$\Delta L = \alpha L \Delta T$

where

  • $\alpha$ is the coefficient of linear expansion
  • $L$ is the length

Similarly, when considering a change in volume,

$\Delta V = \beta V \Delta T$

where

  • $\beta$ is the coefficient of volume expansion
  • $V$ is the volume

Heat Transfer

The rate at which the heat is exchanged is proportional to the temperature gradient

$ \begin{eqnarray} \frac{dQ}{dt} & = & k \int_{S} \hat{n} \cdot \nabla T \, dA \\ \end{eqnarray} $

In a one-dimensional geometry,

$ \begin{eqnarray} \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta T} = k A \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x} \\ \end{eqnarray} $ where

  • $k$ is the thermal consuctivity
  • $A$ is the cross-sectional area

Ideal Gas Law

$ \begin{eqnarray} p \, V & = & n \, R \, T \\ & = & \frac{N}{N_{A}} \, R \, T \\ & = & N \, \frac{R}{N_{A}} \, T \\ & = & N \, k_{B} \, T \\ \end{eqnarray} $ where

  • $n$ is the number of moles of the gas molecules
  • $N$ is the number of molecules of the gas
  • $R$ is the universal gas constant $(8.314472(15) J / mol \cdot K)$
  • $k_{B}$ is Boltzmann's constant $(1.3806488(13) \times 10^{-23} \, J \cdot K^{-1})$
  • $T$ is the absolute temperature

Kinetic Theory of Gases

The pressure of a gases arises from the collisions of molecules of gas with the walls of the container.

$ \begin{eqnarray} K_{avg} & = & \frac{3}{2} \, k_{B} \, T \\ \end{eqnarray} $

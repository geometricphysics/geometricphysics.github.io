Inductance

Self Inductance

A wire carrying an electric current will generate a magnetic flux, $\Phi$, which is proportial to the current, $I$, passing through the wire. The constant of proprtionality is called the inductance, $L$, giving

$ \begin{eqnarray} \Phi & = & L I \\ \end{eqnarray} $.

By the Faraday-Lenz law, a change in the flux linkage will induce a current flow with an e.m.f.

$ \begin{eqnarray} \varepsilon & = & \frac{d \Phi}{d t} \\ \end{eqnarray} $.

Hence, the voltage drop across an inductor is

$ \begin{eqnarray} V & = & L \frac{d I}{d t} \\ \end{eqnarray} $.

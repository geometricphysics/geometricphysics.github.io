Geometric Physics Project Overview

The purpose of this website is to research and prototype content, presentation, interactivity and pedagogy for a Next Generation Geometric Physics Learning Environment. It is, by no means, a complete curriculum resource!

Geometry as Motivating Principle

Nature's Laws tends to be simplest when expressed in terms of geometric quantities.

Is it possible to reformulate physics curriculum to take advantage of this new perspective?

Modeling-based and Inquiry-based Pedagogy as Best Practices

Physics Education Research shows that the traditional cycle of lecture followed by experimental validation and problems is not effective for the majority of beginning physics students. Furthermore, a modeling-based approach has been shown to be more effective.

This suggests that if technology is used as a tool for teaching physics then it must at least have the kinds of features that are present in a modeling-based course with an expert instructor.

Geometric Physics Manifesto

  • Expose the mathematical unity that is obscured by traditional vector analysis.
  • Leverage geometric visualization of duality.
  • Provide a consolidation of Geometric Algebra/Calculus and Differential Forms/Geometry.
  • Pave the way to Maxwell, Gravitation and Quantum Mechanics.
  • Don't dumb down the Math; make it accessible instead.
  • Don't push facts and memorization. Derive theorems, even if limited in scope.
  • Geometric Algebra as the Mathematical Language foundation.
  • Calculus is essential.
  • Motivate by realistic examples and challenging problems.
  • Videos for explaining difficult concepts.
  • Modeling-based instruction
  • Programming to understand mathematical operations, function types and phenomena.

Roadmap

I am researching web-based technologies to create interactive physics content. If that works out well enough, the next step will be to personalize the experience for a student. It may also take quite a bit of (dare I say it?) Artificial Intelligence to arrive at something that can rival, exceed, or compliment the modeling-based and inquiry-based approaches to pedagogy that have been validated by physics education research.
